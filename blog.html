Unlocking Potential:
The Joy of Teaching Music in a Prison Setting

By Ivan Keytickler

Stepping into the prison facility as a music instructor was both daunting and exciting. I had heard stories about the transformative power of art and music, but nothing could have prepared me for the profound impact my first piano lesson with an incarcerated inmate would have on both of us. As the notes resonated through the cold walls, I realized that teaching music in a prison situation was not just about imparting skills; it was about fostering hope, self-discovery, and a renewed sense of purpose.

A Journey of Discovery:

The moment I sat down at the piano, I felt a mixture of anticipation and nervousness. I watched as my student, a young man with a quiet demeanor, took a seat beside me. Little did I know that this would be the start of an incredible journey for both of us...

As I guided him through the basics of piano playing, it became apparent that he possessed a natural talent. His fingers danced across the keys with a fluidity that surprised us both. With every note he played, a spark ignited within him, and I couldn't help but be swept away by his enthusiasm and progress.

The Power of Music in a Confined Space:

Teaching music in a prison setting creates a unique dynamic where the transformative power of art takes on a whole new meaning. In this confined space, music becomes a lifeline—a means of expression, escape, and self-discovery. The act of creating melodies allows inmates to transcend the boundaries of their surroundings, connecting with their innermost emotions and unlocking hidden talents.

For my student, the piano provided solace and a way to temporarily transcend the prison walls. As he immersed himself in the music, I witnessed a profound sense of joy and liberation that comes from creating something beautiful in the midst of adversity. It was a reminder that even in the darkest of circumstances, the human spirit can soar through the power of music.

The Rewards of Teaching:

The joy I experienced as a music instructor in this prison setting was unlike anything I had ever felt. Witnessing the transformation in my student, seeing his self-confidence grow with every lesson, was incredibly rewarding. Music had become a catalyst for positive change, enabling him to discover a sense of purpose and fulfillment.

But the rewards were not one-sided. Teaching in a prison situation has also allowed me to grow as an instructor and as a person. It has reminded me of the transformative power of music and the profound impact it can have on individuals, regardless of their circumstances. It has reaffirmed my belief that everyone, regardless of their past mistakes, deserves the opportunity to heal, grow, and reintegrate into society.

Looking Ahead:

As I continue my journey as a music instructor in the prison system, I am filled with hope and determination. Each lesson presents an opportunity to make a difference in someone's life, to foster personal growth, and to inspire change.

Teaching music in a prison situation is not without its challenges, but the rewards far outweigh any obstacles. It is a privilege to witness the transformative impact of music on incarcerated individuals, to be a part of their journey towards rehabilitation and reintegration.

In the end, my first piano lesson with the incarcerated inmate was not just about teaching the keys and notes; it was about helping him unlock his true potential, reminding him that his past does not define him. Music has become a bridge, connecting us in a shared language of creativity and possibility.

As I leave the prison facility after each lesson, a sense of fulfillment washes over me. I am reminded of the tremendous power of music to transcend boundaries, ignite hope, and inspire change—one key at a time.

Painting My Way to Freedom:
A Journey of Self-Discovery Behind Bars

By Pincel Rojo

While I was an inmate in prison, I embarked on a remarkable journey of self-discovery through painting lessons. These lessons have not only improved my artistic skills but have also completely changed the way I see the world. In this blog post, I want to share my experience of learning to paint and how it has brought color, beauty, and a fresh perspective to my life.

Embracing Creativity Behind Bars:

When I first held a paintbrush, I was unsure and a bit nervous. But as I began putting paint to canvas, I realized it was a place where I could express myself without any judgment or restrictions. Painting became my sanctuary, a space where I could escape from my past and dive into the depths of my imagination...

The Power of Artistic Expression:

Through painting, I found a way to let out my emotions—an avenue to confront and process the ups and downs of my life. The canvas became my best friend, witnessing my struggles, dreams, and hopes. With each brushstroke, I discovered more about myself, gradually healing wounds that were buried deep within.

Seeing Beauty in Imperfections:

As I learned to paint, I also learned to accept imperfections, both in my artwork and in myself. I realized that beauty can be found in flaws and the unique qualities that make us who we are. Each brushstroke, whether carefully planned or spontaneous, reflected my journey—a reminder that life's imperfections make it extraordinary.

Discovering a World Beyond Prison Walls:

With every stroke of the brush, I began to see beyond the concrete walls and barbed wires that surround me. Art allowed me to transport myself to faraway landscapes, bustling cities, and enchanting realms. Painting became a window to freedom, reminding me that there is a world waiting for me beyond these prison confines.

Finding Connection and Healing:

The painting lessons not only helped me grow as an individual but also created a sense of community among fellow inmates. We shared our artworks, gave feedback, and celebrated each other's progress. Through these connections, I discovered that art can bridge gaps, break down barriers, and foster understanding—a reminder that we are all capable of change and transformation.

A Shift in Perspective:

Learning to paint has fundamentally changed the way I see life. It has taught me to look beyond what's on the surface—to notice the play of light and shadows, the harmony in chaos, and the beauty in everyday moments. This new perspective extends beyond art and has influenced how I face challenges, build relationships, and value myself.

A Canvas for Redemption:

As I paint, I am reminded that my past does not define me. Each brushstroke represents a chance for redemption—a testament to my capacity for growth, change, and a brighter future. Art has shown me that mistakes don't define my worth but rather present an opportunity to learn and become a better version of myself.

Conclusion:

Learning to paint while being behind bars has been an incredible journey. Through art, I have found solace, self-expression, and a renewed sense of purpose. Painting has broadened my horizons, facilitated healing, and allowed me to discover beauty within and beyond the prison walls. It is a journey of resilience, personal growth, and the unyielding pursuit of transformation—one brushstroke at a time.

Money Matters:
Earning, Spending, and Saving in Prison

By Devona Freedom

When it comes to finances, life behind bars is a whole different world. In this informative blog post, we will explore how prison inmates make money while incarcerated, what items they can purchase through the commissary, and the challenges they face when it comes to saving money within the prison system.

Earning Money in Prison:

Prison inmates have limited opportunities to earn money while serving their sentences. However, some facilities offer vocational training programs that provide inmates with job skills, enabling them to earn a small wage for their work. These jobs can range from custodial work, kitchen duty, or maintenance tasks within the prison grounds. The wages earned vary between institutions and can be as little as a few cents to a few dollars per hour...

Commissary: A Lifeline for Basic Needs and Comforts:

In most prisons, inmates have access to a commissary—a facility where they can purchase a variety of items using their own funds. The commissary offers essential supplies such as hygiene products, stationery, and clothing items. Inmates can also buy snacks, drinks, and limited food items to supplement the prison meals. The selection and availability of items can vary between facilities, and prices are often higher compared to outside stores.

The Challenge of Saving Money in Prison:

Saving money while in prison can be extremely challenging due to several factors. Firstly, the low wages earned by inmates make it difficult to accumulate substantial savings. Secondly, the limited availability of jobs and the high demand for those positions means that not every inmate has the opportunity to work and earn money.

Additionally, the high cost of basic necessities in the commissary can eat into any potential savings. Many items are priced at a premium, and inmates may face difficult choices when deciding between purchasing essentials or indulging in small comforts.

Furthermore, unexpected expenses, such as medical co-pays or fees for phone calls and emails, can quickly deplete an inmate's funds. These costs are often necessary for maintaining contact with loved ones and accessing vital services, leaving little room for saving money.

Financial Support from the Outside:

In some cases, inmates receive financial support from family or friends outside the prison. These external funds can be deposited into an inmate's account, providing them with additional resources to meet their needs or save money for the future. However, this support is not available to all inmates, and the amount of outside assistance varies widely.

Financial Education and Planning:

Recognizing the challenges inmates face in managing their finances, some prisons offer financial literacy programs to help inmates develop budgeting and money management skills. These programs provide valuable knowledge on topics such as saving, debt management, and planning for reintegration into society. By equipping inmates with financial education, they can make more informed decisions and work towards a stable financial future.

Conclusion:

Navigating finances while incarcerated is no easy task. Inmates have limited earning opportunities and face challenges when it comes to saving money within the prison system. The commissary offers essential items and small comforts but at a higher cost. Additionally, unexpected expenses and the lack of high-paying jobs make saving money a difficult endeavor.

Despite the hurdles, financial literacy programs and external support can offer some relief and guidance to inmates. By providing inmates with the necessary tools and resources, we can empower them to make informed financial decisions and work towards financial stability during and after their time behind bars.
