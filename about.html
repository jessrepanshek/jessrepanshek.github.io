About Jess

About Me

Jess Repanshek, a dynamic web designer and developer, calls Portland, Oregon home. But his journey to this vibrant tech hub is anything but ordinary.

Jess's story begins with music. From a young age, he was strumming the guitar and jamming with bands, soon venturing into the world of electronic music production. This passion led him to the Conservatory of Recording Arts and Sciences in Tempe, Arizona in 2005, where he honed his audio engineering skills. His hands-on training culminated in an internship at the Salt Mine Studio Oasis, a premier recording studio in Mesa, Arizona.

For the next decade, Jess dedicated himself to the Conservatory, evolving from project staff to a revered instructor. He taught a range of subjects, from computer networking and troubleshooting to studio recording and basic electronics. A pivotal moment came in 2008 with the school's 1:1 Student Laptop Initiative, which propelled Jess into the role of IT Administrator.

As the IT Administrator for CRAS’s Gilbert campus from 2009 onward, Jess managed student laptops, maintained both the school's Moodle-based educational website and its internet infrastructure, and provided help desk support to students and staff alike. He was instrumental in developing a new Software Proficiencies program, practical exams that bolstered students' real-world skills and employability in a variety of commonly used audio applications including Avid Pro Tools and Apple Logic Pro.

In 2017, Jess pivoted to a new challenge, joining the Milton H. Erickson Foundation as an IT Specialist. Here, he managed WordPress websites and developed the organization's CE21-based online storefront. During the pandemic, Jess played a crucial role in transitioning the Foundation’s educational offerings to an online format, ensuring continuity and accessibility.

His crowning achievement at the Erickson Foundation was the All Access Membership project. To support this membership program, Jess spearheaded the creation of the Clinical Media Library, digitizing and categorizing hundreds of hours of educational audio and video content. Launched in 2022, this project made rare and valuable resources available to a global audience.

Beyond his professional pursuits, Jess produces weekly podcasts for National Parks Traveler, a non-profit organization. These podcasts, covering diverse topics from national park politics to underwater explorations to wildlife conservation, garner over 10,000 downloads monthly. In 2020, he expanded his contributions by launching a Shopify store for the Traveler, selling custom merchandise and eBooks.
